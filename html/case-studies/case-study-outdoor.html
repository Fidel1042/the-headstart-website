The Company

Summit Edge is an Australian outdoor gear brand founded in 2017. It designs and sells premium hiking jackets, packs, tents, and sleeping bags through its own website and 14 retail stores across Australia. The brand has built a loyal following on durability and a clean, no-nonsense aesthetic.

The Situation

Summit Edge has hit a growth ceiling in the Australian market. Revenue has plateaued at $42M for the past 2 years despite consistent marketing spend. The leadership team wants to expand internationally and New Zealand is the obvious first move (similar climate, similar outdoor culture, no language barrier, free trade agreement).

The CEO has asked two questions: should we enter New Zealand, and if yes, how? The data below was put together by the strategy lead.

Discussion Questions
1
NZ is one of several possible international moves for Summit Edge. Where would you start with this question, and what is the most important thing to figure out before recommending anything? Talk through what you would need to know to make a confident call.
2
How attractive does the NZ market actually look from what you can see? What jumps out from the data, and what does it tell you about the competitive dynamics? Where do you see the biggest opportunity, and where do you see the biggest risk?
3
If Summit Edge decides to enter, there are many different ways in: from a quiet online launch through to acquiring a local brand. Which approaches feel realistic for a company their size, and which one would your group back?
4
What is your group's final call: enter or hold off? Be clear on what would have to be true for your recommendation to play out, and what could derail it.
