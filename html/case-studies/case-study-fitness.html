The Company

FlexFit is a Sydney-based boutique fitness chain founded in 2019. It runs 14 studios offering HIIT, strength, and reformer pilates classes. Members pay $220/month for unlimited access or $35 per class as a drop-in. The brand sits clearly in the premium boutique tier of the market.

The Situation

Member growth has stalled and monthly churn has nearly doubled to 14% over the past 18 months. The leadership team is split. One camp wants to drop prices to win back members. Another wants to raise prices to protect the premium positioning. A third wants to restructure the offer entirely (tiers, off-peak pricing, class packs).

A decision is needed in the next 6 weeks before the next marketing campaign launches. The data below was put together by the operations lead.

Discussion Questions
1
Churn has nearly doubled in 18 months and no one is fully sure why. What is your initial read on what is driving it, and what is the first thing you would want to understand? Compare and challenge each other's initial views before settling on a likely cause.
2
The leadership team thinks this is a pricing problem. Is it really, or is something else going on? Look across the three charts and make the case either way.
3
The leadership team is split between three camps: drop prices, raise prices, or restructure the offer entirely. What are the realistic pricing moves on the table, and which one actually fits FlexFit's situation? If a stronger option sits outside these three, make the case for it.
4
What is your group's final recommendation on pricing, and what is the biggest thing that could go wrong with it? How would you protect against that risk?
