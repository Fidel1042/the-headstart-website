The Company

Cup Above Coffee is a Brisbane-based specialty coffee chain founded in 2019. It runs 8 stores across the inner suburbs, employs around 85 people, and has built a loyal following on locally roasted beans and a small but high-quality food menu. For its first 5 years the business grew steadily and stores were consistently busy.

The Situation

Over the past 18 months, revenue has held roughly flat but net profit has dropped 28%. Operating costs (wages, rent, ingredients) have risen faster than the team can absorb, and margins are getting squeezed every quarter. The founder knows something needs to change but is not sure where to focus first.

Discussion Questions
1
The founder knows something is off but is not sure where to look. What is your initial read on what is actually going on, and where would you start digging? Talk through what feels most likely versus what might be hiding under the surface.
2
Each chart shows a different angle on the business. What story do they tell when you look at them together, and which one do you think matters most? Is there anything that surprises you, or anything that does not quite add up?
3
The founder has some cash to invest but limited time and attention. What are the realistic ways they could respond? Push past the obvious answers and consider both quick fixes and bigger plays.
4
If you had to give the founder one clear direction to take, what would it be and why? What makes your answer better than the alternatives the group considered?
