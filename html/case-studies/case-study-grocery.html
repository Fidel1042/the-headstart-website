The Company

GreenGo is a Sydney-based online grocery delivery service founded in 2022. It focuses on premium, organic, and locally sourced products with a 2-hour delivery window. Average basket size is $45 and the team handles around 8,000 orders per week. The 60-person team includes drivers, warehouse staff, and a small head office.

The Situation

Eight weeks ago, FreshDash, a US grocery delivery giant backed by $200M in venture funding, launched in Sydney. They offer 30-minute delivery, prices around 30% lower than GreenGo's, and roughly 3x the product range. GreenGo's weekly orders have dropped 23% since the launch.

The founder team has 30 days to decide how to respond before investor reporting season. The data below was put together over the weekend.

Discussion Questions
1
FreshDash arrived eight weeks ago and the impact has been immediate. What is your initial read on GreenGo's position: do they have a real fight on their hands, or is this already lost? Be honest about what the situation actually looks like.
2
GreenGo cannot beat FreshDash at everything. Where do they have a genuine edge they can build on, and where are they most exposed? Use the charts to back up your view.
3
The founder team has been weighing several options this week: lower prices, narrow the range, lean into quality, partner with a major retailer, or even sell to FreshDash. What are the realistic ways they could respond, and which one is the most promising? Reach a position as a group.
4
What is your group's recommendation, and what is it asking the founder to bet on? How would you justify that bet to investors at the next board meeting?
