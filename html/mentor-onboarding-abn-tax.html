ABN & Tax Setup

What Is an ABN?

An ABN (Australian Business Number) is an 11-digit number that identifies you as operating a business in Australia. As a Headstart mentor, you are paid as a contractor, not an employee. To receive payment from us legally, you must have an ABN and include it on every invoice you send.

If you do not provide an ABN, Australian tax law requires us to withhold 47% of your payment and send it to the ATO. That is not a penalty - it is the default tax rate applied when no ABN is present. Registering avoids this entirely.

How to Register Your ABN

Registration is free and takes around 15 minutes. Your ABN is issued instantly in most cases.

  1. 1 Go to the Australian Business Register link below and click “Apply for an ABN”
  2. 2 Select entity type: Sole Trader (this is correct for individual contractors)
  3. 3 Fill in your personal details. For business activity, enter: mentoring services or educational consulting
  4. 4 Submit. Your ABN will appear on screen immediately and be emailed to you
  5. 5 You do not need to register a business name. Your legal name is sufficient

Your Tax Obligations

Having an ABN does not mean tax is automatically handled. No tax is withheld from what Headstart pays you. You are responsible for managing your own tax obligations.

  • Lodge a tax return each year. All income earned under your ABN is declared as business income in your individual tax return. It is added to any other income you have, such as wages from a part-time job.
  • Set money aside for tax. A rough guide based on your total annual income:
Total Annual Income Approx. Tax Rate to Set Aside
Under $18,200 0% (tax-free threshold)
$18,201 to $45,000 ~19%
$45,001 to $120,000 ~32.5%
Note on GST

You only need to register for GST if your total business income across all work exceeds $75,000 per year. Most mentors will not reach this threshold. Do not register for GST unless you hit that figure.

Superannuation

Super is Australia’s retirement savings system. Employees have super paid on top of their wages by their employer. As a contractor, you manage your own super.

You are not required to open a new super fund. Most Australians already have one from a previous or current job. If you don’t have one yet, the ATO will assign you a default fund called a Stapled Super Fund when you first start earning.

The Contractor Super Rule

Under Australian law, if a contract is wholly or principally for a person’s labour, the business paying the contractor may be required to contribute super on their behalf at the current rate of 11.5%. This rule can apply to mentoring arrangements. We are working through our obligations here and will update you directly. In the meantime, consider making voluntary super contributions from your earnings yourself. A common approach is to put 11.5% of what you earn from mentoring directly into your super fund.

What You Need to Send Us

Before your first payment, send us the following:

  • Your ABN so we can verify it and keep it on file
  • Your super fund name and member number so we can pay super contributions on your behalf if required

For every session you invoice us for, your invoice must include: your full name, your ABN, description of service (e.g. “1x mentoring session”), the amount, and the date.
